The animation successfully displays a right-angled triangle constructs squares on each side and presents the equation (a^2 + b^2 = c^2). However it does not provide a true geometric proof of the theorem because the areas are not actually rearranged or transformed to show equality. Instead the smaller squares disappear and are replaced by the larger square which weakens the mathematical justification. The right-angle condition is also not emphasized clearly throughout the animation making it less obvious that the theorem applies specifically to right triangles. Although different colours are used for the three squares their meaning is never explicitly explained reducing the effectiveness of the visual design. Finally the transition from the area demonstration to the final equation is abrupt .

The Fourier series animation effectively shows the sequential addition of odd harmonics and illustrates how the cumulative waveform approaches a square wave. However the axis labels and numerical markings make the graph difficult to read in some sections. The cumulative sum labels become excessively long as more harmonics are added causing unnecessary crowding on the screen. Some annotations and labels compete for space with the plotted curves and legend reducing overall readability. In addition the final approximation curve is not highlighted strongly enough relative to the individual harmonics making it harder to distinguish the most important result of the animation. The animation also lacks explanatory text describing why only odd harmonics are used and how each added harmonic improves the approximation limiting its educational value for viewers who are unfamiliar with Fourier series.
